Must Know Quick Reference

Week 1 Content

Anatomy & Early Development

  • Umbilical cord: 2 arteries (carry waste away), 1 vein (carries oxygenated blood to the baby) — it's "backwards." On an exam, you want more A's.
  • Quickening (fetal movement felt by mom) by about 20 weeks.

Genetics

  • You will have to build and interpret a Punnett square; the question will tell you if the disorder is dominant or recessive.
  • Know how traits are expressed: dominant needs 1 abnormal allele; recessive needs 2.

Signs, Dating & Assessment

  • Naegele's Rule (you will calculate it): first day of LMP − 3 months + 7 days.
  • GTPAL — Gravida counts pregnancies, not babies (twins = one pregnancy), and includes the current one.
  • Fundal height should be within about 2 cm of gestational age in weeks.
  • Signs of pregnancy: presumptive (subjective) vs. probable (objective, e.g. a positive pregnancy test) vs. positive (only explained by pregnancy, e.g. the HCP palpates fetal movement).

Infections, Disorders & IPV

  • For each breast/gyn disorder and STI: know etiology, symptoms, diagnosis, treatment, and complications. (See the Disorders Quick Reference table below.)
  • Genital herpes (HSV-2): start acyclovir at 36 weeks; an active lesion at delivery = C-section.
  • Intimate partner violence: know what to do if it's disclosed — screen privately, do not mention it in front of others, provide resources / a safety plan.

Week 2 Content

Hypertensive Disorders

  • ★ Magnesium sulfate in preeclampsia is given to PREVENT SEIZURES — not to lower blood pressure. ("I want 0% of you to get it wrong.")
  • Mag therapeutic level: 4 to 7.
  • Mag toxicity: absent reflexes, decreased respiratory rate, decreased level of consciousness / slurred speech. Antidote: IV calcium gluconate. (Expected side effects = flushing, warmth, drowsiness.)
  • Acute severe HTN meds — know drug, route, and contraindication (not doses): IV labetalol (avoid in asthma — "labetalol, lungs"); IV hydralazine (avoid in tachycardia); oral nifedipine (avoid in tachycardia).
  • Low-dose aspirin 81 mg for anyone at risk for preeclampsia (started 12 to 28 weeks).
  • Diagnosis of hypertension = TWO readings of 140/90 or higher, at least 4 hours apart.
  • Minimum urine output at least 30 mL/hr — be able to calculate it.

Diabetes in Pregnancy

  • ★ Gestational diabetes glucose goals: fasting under 95, one hour under 140, two hours under 120.
  • 3-hour glucose tolerance test: 2 of 4 values elevated = gestational diabetes (don't memorize the exact cutoffs).

Fetal Testing & Screening

  • ★ NST reactive criteria: 32 weeks or more = 2 accelerations of at least 15 bpm above baseline lasting 15 sec within 20 min; under 32 weeks = 10 bpm for 10 sec.
  • ★ CST is "backwards": Negative = reassuring (no late decelerations); Positive = bad (late decels with more than half of contractions).
  • Biophysical profile (BPP) components — BATMAN: Breathing, Amniotic fluid, Tone, Movement, plus the Non-Stress Test.
  • BPP scoring: each component is 0 or 2 (never a 1); if the score is not 8/8, do a non-stress test.
  • L/S ratio of 2:1 or higher = mature fetal lungs.
  • Quad screen / MSAFP: maternal blood at about 15 to 22 weeks; it is a SCREEN, not diagnostic.
  • AFP: HIGH = open neural tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly), also multiples/diabetes; LOW = Down syndrome or trisomy 18.

Maternal Nutrition

  • ★ Folic acid: memorize at least 400 mcg/day (during pregnancy or when trying to conceive).

Cardiac Disorders

  • Focus on signs of cardiac decompensation and reducing cardiac workload — not the individual disorders.

Week 3 Content

Labor & Birth (Intrapartum I)

  • ★ Fetal position from a picture: she said she "could show you on a quiz or an exam" a picture and ask for the three-letter position notation. Read it in order: side (R/L of the mother) · landmark (Occiput, Mentum, Sacrum, Acromion) · direction (Anterior, Posterior, Transverse). Find the landmark first. Occiput anterior (e.g., ROA) is the most favorable — the baby is born face-down.
  • Station: the ischial spines are zero station and are the narrowest part of the pelvis; above = negative (−1 to −5), below = positive (+1 to +5). +4 = birth is imminent (the head is visible).
  • Dilation 0–10 cm; effacement 0–100%. Never push before 10 cm (fully dilated) — pushing on an incomplete cervix can tear it or make it swell.
  • True labor = progressive cervical change. The stage/phase is judged by dilation (latent 0–3, active 4–7, transition 8–10); engagement = 0 station.
  • Contraction frequency = start of one contraction to the start of the next (in minutes).
  • Third stage ≤ 30 min (longer = hemorrhage / retained placenta risk); give oxytocin after the placenta delivers, then fundal massage.
  • 4th stage: a soft/boggy fundus is uterine atony — the big postpartum-hemorrhage red flag.

Fetal Monitoring (Intrapartum II)

  • VEAL CHOP: Variables = Cord compression · Early = Head compression · Accelerations = Okay · Late = Placental insufficiency. She teaches interventions with UNCOIL: change position (left side), Oxygen / turn Oxytocin off, IV fluid bolus (LR), Lower the head of the bed.
  • ★ Accelerations — she said she will always give you the gestational age: ≥ 32 weeks = 15 bpm above baseline for 15 sec; < 32 weeks = 10 bpm for 10 sec.
  • Variability is the BEST indicator of fetal oxygenation (moderate 6–25 is what you want; absent is ominous / category III).
  • Decelerations by cause: early = head compression (benign — do a vaginal exam, the baby's getting low); late = uteroplacental insufficiency (a perfusion problem — always fix); variable = cord compression (reposition first; think nuchal cord); prolonged (≥2 min, <10) → think prolapsed cord or abruption.
  • Sinusoidal pattern = the baby may be acidotic → deliver, notify the provider immediately.
  • Fetal tachycardia (>160 for ≥10 min): check the maternal temperature FIRST (rule out infection/fever).
  • Montevideo units (IUPC): peak minus resting baseline, summed over 10 min — want >200 (≤300) for adequate labor; resting tone <25; tachysystole = ≥6 contractions in 10 min.
  • Best place to hear the FHR = over the fetal shoulders/back (use Leopold's to find it). For ROA, listen in the RLQ.
  • GBS: penicillin during labor (drug of choice); clindamycin 900 mg IV q4h if penicillin-allergic. Terbutaline for tachysystole — hold if maternal HR ≥120.
  • Triage tip: if she's thrashing and the toco isn't registering contractions, palpate the fundus first (rule out abruption/rupture) before readjusting the monitor or medicating.

Not Tested

She said you do NOT need to memorize these

  • Which genetic disorders are dominant vs. recessive (the question tells you).
  • The four quad-screen substances / what MSAFP screens for.
  • The exact 3-hour glucose tolerance test cutoffs.
  • Exact doses of the severe-hypertension drugs.
  • The exact amount each nutrient need increases (except folic acid, 400 mcg).
  • The specific cardiac disorders.
  • Detailed severe-preeclampsia lab thresholds.
