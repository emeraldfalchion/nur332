Fibrocystic breast changes Likely estrogen/progesterone imbalance; most common benign breast disorder Mobile, tender lumps/cysts; worse before menses Mammogram, fine-needle aspiration Limit caffeine & sodium; oral contraceptives None significant (benign) — a FIXED mass with skin retraction is the cancer red flag, not this

Bacterial Vaginosis Decreased normal vaginal flora (frequent sex, douching) Thin, watery, gray discharge; fishy odor Slide prep; vaginal pH greater than 4.5 Metronidazole (Flagyl), clindamycin cream Increases risk of acquiring an STI
Vaginal Candidiasis Antibiotics, OCPs, immunosuppression, diabetes Thick, curd-like white discharge; severe itching, rash Spores on microscopy Fluconazole (Diflucan) or nystatin; avoid Diflucan in pregnancy (use topical agents) Can be passed between partners
Trichomoniasis Trichomonas vaginalis (most common non-viral STI) Yellow-green discharge, inflammation, itching, dysuria Visualization on wet mount Metronidazole; non-treatment trending in pregnancy Preterm labor risk
Chlamydia Bacterial STI; often asymptomatic Thin purulent discharge, dysuria, lower abdominal pain Culture of vaginal secretions Azithromycin Untreated: neonatal conjunctivitis, blindness, pneumonia
Gonorrhea Bacterial STI Purulent greenish-yellow discharge, dysuria, vulvar swelling Culture Ceftriaxone (Rocephin) + azithromycin Untreated: neonatal blindness/eye problems; can cause PID
Herpes Simplex (HSV-1/HSV-2) Herpes simplex virus Single or clustered vesicles; itching/tingling before outbreak Lesion culture Acyclovir (suppresses, no cure); start at 36 wks gestation Active lesion at delivery: C-section required; fatal neonatal infection risk
Human Papillomavirus (HPV) Viral STI (vaginal, oral, or anal transmission) Genital warts, or none Biopsy; abnormal Pap smear Cryotherapy, shave/acid removal; Gardasil vaccine (prevention) Leading cause of cervical cancer; long incubation (years)
Syphilis T. pallidum, contact with open wound; can be congenital Chancre to wart-like plaque; fever, weight loss, malaise VDRL or RPR (RPR preferred) Penicillin Congenital transmission: IUGR, preterm birth, stillbirth
Pelvic Inflammatory Disease Risk factors: multiple partners, IUD use, untreated gonorrhea/chlamydia Bilateral sharp cramping pain, fever, chills, purulent discharge; can be asymptomatic Vaginal culture, CBC, VDRL/RPR (rule out syphilis) Multi-antibiotic combination Tubal damage, infertility
Urinary Tract Infections Cystitis (lower): E. coli/staph. Pyelonephritis (upper): ascending, untreated lower UTI Cystitis: low-grade fever, hematuria, dysuria. Pyelonephritis: high fever, chills, unilateral flank pain Urine culture (both) Cystitis: oral antibiotics per culture. Pyelonephritis: IV fluids/antibiotics ± pain meds Untreated: can ascend to pyelonephritis; increases preterm labor risk

Sprint Review Flashcards

How many days is a normal menstrual cycle?
28 days
What happens on Day 1 of the menstrual cycle?
Bleeding begins
During what days of the cycle is a woman most fertile?
Days 13–15
Describe vaginal secretions during the fertile period.
Thin and watery
Where do sperm collect once deposited in the female reproductive tract?
The posterior fornix
Where is the site of fertilization?
The ampulla of the fallopian tube
For how long is the egg fertile after ovulation?
About 12–24 hours
For how long is sperm fertile after ejaculation?
Viable 48–72 hours, but most fertile around 24 hours
Where is the site of implantation of the fertilized egg?
The endometrium
What hormone is released after the fertilized egg implants?
hCG
Where are the fallopian tubes ligated to ensure sterility?
The isthmus
What two membranes surround the fetus in utero?
The chorion and the amnion
What term describes too much amniotic fluid?
Hydramnios (polyhydramnios)
What term describes too little amniotic fluid?
Oligohydramnios
How many arteries and veins are in the umbilical cord?
2 arteries, 1 vein
How many weeks pregnant is a woman on her due date?
40 weeks
At the start of how many weeks is a pregnancy considered term?
37 weeks
At the start of how many weeks is a pregnancy considered viable?
24 weeks
At about how many weeks does a mother typically first feel fetal movement?
20 weeks (may be earlier for experienced mothers)
In a normal karyotype, what is the total number of chromosomes?
46 (23 pairs)
In a normal karyotype, how many are autosomes?
44 (22 pairs)
In a normal karyotype, how many are sex chromosomes?
2 (1 pair)
Using B/b notation, what genotype is homozygous dominant? Homozygous recessive?
Homozygous dominant = BB. Homozygous recessive = bb.
Using B/b notation, what genotype is heterozygous?
Bb
For an autosomal dominant disorder, which genotype(s) show the condition — and can someone be a silent carrier?
BB and Bb both show the condition. No — there's no silent carrier state for an autosomal dominant disorder.
For an autosomal recessive disorder, which genotype shows the condition — and can someone be a carrier?
Only bb shows the condition. Yes — Bb is an unaffected carrier.
Practice problem: Polydactyly is autosomal dominant. A mother who is heterozygous (Bb) has a child with a father who is homozygous affected (BB). What's the chance their child has polydactyly?
100% — every child gets at least one dominant allele from the homozygous BB parent, so all offspring are BB or Bb and show the trait.
Practice problem: Cystic fibrosis is autosomal recessive. Both parents are carriers (Bb × Bb). What are the odds for their child?
25% affected (bb), 50% carrier (Bb), 25% unaffected (BB)
During pregnancy, does the incidence of intimate partner violence (IPV) increase or decrease?
Increases
What is Goodell's sign?
Softening of the cervix
What is Hegar's sign?
Softening of the uterus
What is Chadwick's sign?
Bluish discoloration of the cervix
Which hormone is primarily responsible for relaxing body structures in pregnancy?
Progesterone
What is colostrum?
The nutrient-dense first milk substance produced by the breasts
What type of breathing do expectant mothers typically use by the third trimester?
Thoracic breathing
What condition is caused by the gravid uterus compressing the vena cava, causing a drop in maternal blood pressure?
Supine hypotension syndrome
Is a hematocrit slightly lower than non-pregnancy levels normal or abnormal in pregnancy?
Normal
Is shortness of breath at rest in the first trimester normal or abnormal?
Abnormal — warrants further assessment
Is an increase in clotting factors normal or abnormal in pregnancy?
Normal
Is hyperpigmentation across the cheeks and forehead normal or abnormal in pregnancy?
Normal (chloasma, the "mask of pregnancy")
Is separation of the rectus abdominis muscles normal or abnormal in pregnancy?
Normal (diastasis recti)
What's the total recommended weight gain for a woman with a normal pre-pregnancy BMI?
25–35 lb
Fetal movement felt by the pregnant woman — presumptive, probable, or positive sign?
Presumptive
Fetal movement seen on ultrasound — presumptive, probable, or positive sign?
Positive
A positive pregnancy test — presumptive, probable, or positive sign?
Probable
Uterine souffle — presumptive, probable, or positive sign?
Probable
Urinary frequency — presumptive, probable, or positive sign?
Presumptive
Case study: A patient at 36 2/7 weeks is admitted for high blood pressure and delivers today. Her history includes one miscarriage, one vaginal delivery at term, and a C-section of twins at 35 weeks for preeclampsia. What's her GTPAL?
G4 T1 P2 A1 L4 — Gravida 4 (this pregnancy plus 3 prior); Term 1 (the vaginal term delivery); Preterm 2 (the twin C-section at 35 wks + today's delivery at 36 2/7 wks); Abortions 1 (the miscarriage); Living 4 (1 from the term delivery + 2 twins + today's baby)
Using Naegele's rule, what is the EDC for a patient with an LMP of January 30th?
November 6 (subtract 3 months → October 30, add 7 days → November 6)
At how many weeks do you begin measuring fundal height, and how close should it track gestational age?
Starting around 20 weeks, tracking within about ±2 cm of gestational age in weeks
What method should be used to calculate a due date when the LMP is uncertain?
Ultrasound
Why should the rubella (MMR) vaccine not be given during pregnancy?
It's a live vaccine
At what gestational age is a patient tested for Group B Strep?
36 weeks
