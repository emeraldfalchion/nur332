Antepartum Care

Intimate partner violence is the most common form of violence against women, and pregnancy increases its incidence. Screen privately, without the partner present, and provide resources discreetly (e.g., pamphlets in patient-only restrooms).

Psychological & Social Adaptation

Maternal Emotional Responses

  • Ambivalence — contradictory emotions (happy but fearful)
  • Acceptance — how she feels about being pregnant
  • Introversion — focus turns inward, onto how pregnancy affects her
  • Mood swings
  • Fear — of the unknown, of a repeat difficult pregnancy, of labor pain
  • Body image changes — can be harder if there's a prior eating disorder or poor body image

Rubin's Tasks of Pregnancy

  • Ensure safe passage through pregnancy, labor, birth
  • Seek acceptance of the child by others
  • Seek commitment/acceptance of self as mother ("binding in")
  • Learn to give of oneself on behalf of the child

Partner response: may feel confused by mood changes, left out, or resentful of the attention shift — include partners in teaching/visits. Couvade syndrome: partner experiences pregnancy-like symptoms (fatigue, sleep trouble, weight gain). Siblings may feel rivalry/fear of changing parental attention and need reassurance. Grandparents may grow closer, but their role should be clarified so the patient's own preferences are centered.

Antepartal History & GTPAL

A thorough prenatal history covers: current/past pregnancies, gynecologic and medical history (incl. substance use), family medical history, religious/spiritual/cultural/occupational history, partner history and health, and psychosocial factors (abuse history, mood disorders, intendedness of the pregnancy, feeding plans).

Dating the Pregnancy

MethodHow It Works
Naegele's RuleFirst day of LMP − 3 months + 7 days. Example: LMP Sept 1 → EDB = June 8.
EDB wheelDial tool — point to the LMP date and read the corresponding due date/current gestational age
McDonald's method (fundal height)Symphysis pubis → fundus, in cm, correlates with weeks gestation between 22–34 weeks (±2 cm). At ~20 weeks the fundus is roughly at the umbilicus.

Self-Test Flashcards

Click a card to flip it and check your answer.

What causes supine hypotensive syndrome, and how do you prevent it?
The uterus compresses the vena cava when lying flat, reducing venous return. Prevent with a lateral tilt, starting around 20 weeks.
Why do pregnant women develop physiologic (pseudo) anemia?
Plasma volume increases faster than red blood cell count, which dilutes the concentration of RBCs
Is a positive pregnancy test a presumptive, probable, or positive sign of pregnancy?
Probable — it's objective, but false positives are possible, so it's not diagnostic on its own. (Presumptive = subjective, like nausea. Positive = only explainable by pregnancy.)
What are the three positive/diagnostic signs of pregnancy?
Auscultation of the fetal heartbeat, fetal movement felt by the examiner, and visualization of the fetus via ultrasound
What are Rubin's four tasks of pregnancy?
Ensure safe passage; seek acceptance of the child by others; seek acceptance of self as mother ("binding in"); learn to give of oneself for the child
Why should you interview a pregnant patient about possible abuse without her partner in the room — and what do you do if she discloses?
Patients in a controlling relationship often won't disclose abuse if their partner is present, so private screening time is essential. If she discloses: don't mention it in front of others, and connect her to resources and a safety plan.
How do you calculate an estimated due date using Naegele's Rule?
Take the FIRST day of the last menstrual period, subtract 3 months, then add 7 days
In GTPAL, what exactly does Gravida count — and how do twins figure in?
Gravida counts PREGNANCIES, not babies, and includes the current one. A twin pregnancy is still Gravida 1 — but it produces 2 Living children.
Between which weeks does fundal height (McDonald's method) correlate most reliably with gestational age, and how close should it be?
22–34 weeks, and it should be within about ±2 cm of gestational age in weeks — more than 2 cm off is a flag
At about how many weeks should a mother feel fetal movement (quickening), and why does it matter?
About 20 weeks — it's a major milestone and a key indicator of fetal well-being, so knowing when it should start helps you spot a concern
A patient's quad screen (MSAFP) comes back abnormal. What do you teach her about what that result means?
It's a maternal blood SCREEN drawn at about 15–22 weeks — not a diagnosis. An abnormal result means follow-up testing (ultrasound, amniocentesis) is needed, not that the baby has a defect.
A pregnant patient reports facial swelling and epigastric pain. What should you be concerned about?
Possible preeclampsia — both are danger signs that need provider evaluation
